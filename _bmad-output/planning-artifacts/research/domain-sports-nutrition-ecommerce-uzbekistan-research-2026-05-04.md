## Regulatory Requirements

### Applicable Regulations

**E-Commerce Legal Framework**

- **Law on E-Commerce No. ZRU-792** (September 29, 2022, effective December 31, 2022) — the primary legal act governing online commerce in Uzbekistan. Stipulates that personal data terms in e-commerce may be contractually determined.
- **Cabinet Resolution No. 885** (December 26, 2024) — "On Measures for the Further Development of E-Commerce in Uzbekistan." Establishes a notification-based regulatory framework effective **July 1, 2025**. Key obligations:
  - E-commerce operators must be **resident legal entities** of Uzbekistan — foreign-registered entities cannot directly operate an e-commerce platform
  - Must comply with laws on e-commerce, personal data protection, copyright, consumer rights, and advertising
  - Must **integrate information systems with the Tax Committee and Customs Committee** of Uzbekistan
  - Must use **separate bank accounts** for e-commerce transactions
  - Self-employed individual income in e-commerce must be recorded in separate accounts

**Supplement Import Regulations**

- **Import permit required** before any food supplement can be sold in Uzbekistan — lead time: **5–8 months**, valid for **5 years** (renewal required every 5 years)
- **Sanitary-epidemiological conclusion** required from health authorities
- **Certificate of conformity** from an ATR-accredited body — valid up to **3 years**
- Labeling: Mandatory Uzbek-language marking on imports was abolished January 1, 2024, however a sanitary-epidemiological conclusion and certificate of conformity still **cannot be issued** without Uzbek-language labeling attached by the manufacturer or importer
- Standards framework: Based on legacy **GOST standards** — not yet fully harmonized with WTO SPS Agreement or Codex Alimentarius

_Source: [Settle Advisory — New E-Commerce Regulations July 2025](https://settleadvisory.com/news-en/new-regulations-for-e-commerce-in-uzbekistan-from-july-1-2025/) | [MSP Corporate — Supplement Registration UZ](https://mspcorporate.com/state-registration-of-food-supplements-uzbekistan.html) | [trade.gov — Uzbekistan Standards](https://www.trade.gov/country-commercial-guides/uzbekistan-standards-trade)_

### Data Protection and Privacy

- **Law No. ZRU-547 "On Personal Data"** (July 2, 2019, effective October 1, 2019) — primary data protection law
- **Data localization requirement (since April 2021):** Foreign companies must store personal data of Uzbek citizens **within Uzbekistan's territory**. Hosting infrastructure must be Uzbekistan-based or compliant.
- **Standard Procedure for Processing Personal Data** approved November 15, 2023 (Order of the Minister of Justice) — specifies operational data handling requirements
- **Breach notification:** Data controllers must notify authorities and affected individuals promptly in case of a breach posing risk to rights and freedoms
- **Data subject rights:** Access to personal data; right to request correction of inaccurate/incomplete data
- **E-commerce intersection:** Resolution No. 885 explicitly requires e-commerce operators to comply with personal data protection legislation

_Practical implication for platform builder:_ User account data, order history, and payment data must be stored on servers physically located in Uzbekistan. Cloud hosting must either use a local provider or a compliant international provider with Uzbekistan data residency.

### Licensing and Certification

- **Business registration:** LLC is the most common structure; registrable online; 100% foreign ownership permitted; no general minimum capital requirement
- **E-commerce operator notification:** Required under Resolution No. 885 from July 1, 2025 — notification-based (not a license), but mandatory
- **No special license** required to operate an e-commerce retail platform for supplements (beyond standard business registration and e-commerce notification)
- **Per-product compliance:** Import permit, sanitary conclusion, and certificate of conformity are product-level certifications, not platform-level licenses
- **Tax registration:** E-commerce operators must integrate with the Tax Committee system; separate bank accounts mandatory

### Implementation Considerations

1. **Register as a resident LLC first** — required before operating an e-commerce platform. Online registration is fast (1–2 weeks). 100% foreign ownership is allowed.
2. **Wholesale supplier partnership is the fastest path to compliance** — uzbeknaturals.com or similar licensed importers can supply pre-certified products, eliminating the 5–8 month import permit bottleneck.
3. **Data localization is non-negotiable** — choose a hosting provider with Uzbekistan data residency from day one (e.g., local VPS providers in Tashkent or compliant cloud zones).
4. **E-commerce notification (July 2025 deadline)** — file the required notification with the relevant authority and configure Tax/Customs system integration before or at launch.
5. **Uzbek-language labeling** — ensure all product listings and any physical packaging include Uzbek-language descriptions to satisfy certificate requirements.
6. **Payment infrastructure** — integrate with Click.uz and Payme (dominant payment gateways); use a dedicated bank account for e-commerce transactions as required by Resolution No. 885.
